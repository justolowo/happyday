2.1 Question 1

The Cars93 dataset in the MASS package contains data from 93 cars on sale in the USA in 1993. Randomly select 40 cars as the training dataset and the remaining 53 cars as the test dataset. Save both datasets as csv files. Find out the number of USA and non-USA cars in the training dataset. (10 pt.)

2.1.1 Solution 1

library(MASS)
df <- Cars93

set.seed(100)
nrows <- nrow(df)
id <- 1:nrows
train.index <- sort(sample(1:nrows,40))
train.index
##  [1]  6  8 11 12 15 16 18 22 23 24 27 28 29 31 32 36 39 40 42 43 47 51 52
## [24] 53 55 58 59 61 62 65 67 71 72 73 74 78 83 86 89 93
test.index <- id[-train.index]
test.index
##  [1]  1  2  3  4  5  7  9 10 13 14 17 19 20 21 25 26 30 33 34 35 37 38 41
## [24] 44 45 46 48 49 50 54 56 57 60 63 64 66 68 69 70 75 76 77 79 80 81 82
## [47] 84 85 87 88 90 91 92
train.df <- df[train.index,]
# library(knitr)

test.df <- df[test.index,]
table(train.df$Origin)

2.3 Question 3

Assume that the probabilities of boy and girl births are 0.515 and 0.485, respectively. Whatâs the probability of having exactly 3 boys out of 10 births? Whatâs the probability of having 8 or more boys out of 10 births? Plot both the density function and cumulative probability function of the number of boy births out of 10. (15 pt.)

prob1 <- dbinom(3,size = 10,prob = 0.515)
prob1
## [1] 0.1034654
prob2 <- 1 - pbinom(7,size = 10,prob = 0.515)
prob2
## [1] 0.06605068
pbinom(7,size = 10,prob = 0.515,lower.tail = FALSE)
## [1] 0.06605068
n <- 10
x <- 0:n
y <- dbinom(x,size = n,prob = 0.515)
plot(x,y)

plot(x,y,type="b",pch=16,col="black", xlab="number of boys in 10 births",ylab="probability")

y <- pbinom(x,size = n,prob = 0.515)
plot(x,y)

plot(x,y,type="b",pch=16,col="black", xlab="number of boys in 10 births",ylab="probability")
